‚ö† Gaps and areas for consideration

No Scope 1 emissions addressed

While likely minimal for a consultancy, any company vehicles, backup generators, or gas heating should be considered. Even if negligible, documenting this decision strengthens audit readiness.

Water usage not mentioned

ISO 14001 typically expects water as an environmental aspect. Even in a small office, documenting why it's excluded (or adding basic monitoring) would be prudent.

Business travel emissions

As a consultancy, client visits and conferences likely generate Scope 3 emissions. This isn't explicitly captured in R1-R6 or objectives.

Remote working environmental impact

With hybrid working mentioned, employee home energy use could be considered a Scope 3 factor. Increasingly expected in sustainability reporting.

Biodiversity and land use

Not relevant for most digital consultancies, but worth a documented exclusion rationale for completeness.

Client project environmental impact

The environmental footprint of systems Storm ID builds for clients (AI workloads, data processing) could be considered under Scope 3, particularly relevant given AI-first positioning.

6. Quick wins to strengthen this register

1
Add a baseline data column

For each KPI, record the starting value and date. Without baselines, you can't demonstrate improvement.

2
Fix the reference inconsistency

Change "O1, O5, O6" references in objectives to "EOP1, EOP5, EOP6" to match the opportunities register.

3
Add action status tracking

For "Further Actions Required", add columns for: Due Date, Status (Not Started / In Progress / Complete), and Evidence Reference.

4
Add business travel as R7

Simple addition: "Emissions from business travel" with KPI "Travel emissions per FTE" - particularly relevant for consultancy work.

5
Document scope exclusions

Add a "Scope & Exclusions" section stating why water, biodiversity, etc. are excluded - auditors appreciate reasoned exclusions.
